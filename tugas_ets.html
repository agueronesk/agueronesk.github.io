Student Project Award - Meet The Winner

Meet Regenerative Salt Landscapes, one of the winning projects of the Students Project Awards! Regenerative Salt Landscapes by:

  • Ezequiel Lopez (@ezequiel.lqz)
  • María Victoria Echegaray (@victoriaechegaray__)
  • Agustina Durandez (@agusdurandez)
  • Universidad Nacional de Córdoba @uncoficial

Located in Olaroz Salt Flat in Jujuy, Argentina, the project addresses a landscape shared by lithium extraction and increasing environmental fragility. Instead of opposing industry, the proposal turns existing infrastructures into regeneration catalysts, transforming the territory into a diversified productive ecosystem.

China

Chaka Salt Lake Tourist Railway Station

Chaka Salt Lake Tourist Railway Station organizes transport, leisure, and viewing functions within an elevated platform structure overlooking the salt lake. The project creates open circulation and public spaces that connect arrival, movement, and landscape. A prefabricated steel structure combined with a membrane roof forms a continuous curved surface that defines the station's identity.

Poland

ECR Health Care Complex

Wave 4 & 5 at the ECR Health Care Complex organize laboratories, clinics, and hospital spaces within a series of volumes shaped by coastal references. The design prioritizes patient comfort, integrating medical functions within environments that reduce the perception of institutional space. Perforated aluminum panels wrap the façades, controlling light and ventilation, while prefabricated balconies and landscaped areas connect the buildings to the surrounding site.

Ukraine

Prostir Business Hub

Prostir Business Hub organizes logistics, office, and commercial functions through a modular system of pitched volumes. The composition transitions from smaller gabled units to larger industrial scales, establishing a consistent architectural rhythm across the site. Reinforced concrete and steel structures are combined with sandwich panels and aluminum composite façades, allowing adaptable internal configurations.

House

Passive House Forest Retreat

Passive House Forest Retreat is designed as a high-performance residence within a forested site in British Columbia. The building follows Passive House principles, combining energy efficiency with a layout oriented toward views and natural light. Dark cladding, precise detailing, and expansive glazing define the exterior, while cantilevered volumes extend toward the landscape.

Veil Adapts a 1950s Residential Building

PVeil adapts a 1950s residential building in Athens through the addition of a new volume that respects the existing urban fabric while introducing contemporary living spaces. The project maintains setbacks to create outdoor areas and preserve spatial relationships with the street. A double-skin façade of perforated aluminum panels regulates light, ventilation, and privacy, while preserved elements such as marble, wood flooring, and traditional frames are combined with new interventions.

Ojo de Nila House

Ojo de Nila House opens fully toward the Pacific Ocean, organizing its spaces to maintain a continuous relationship with the surrounding landscape. The layout removes barriers between interior and exterior, allowing natural airflow and views to define daily use. A lightweight structure supports a curved roof that provides shade and ventilation, while materials such as teak and metal respond to the coastal climate.
