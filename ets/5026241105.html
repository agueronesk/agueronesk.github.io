Student Project Award - Meet the Winner

Meet Regenerative Salt Landscapes, one of the winning projects of the Students Project Awards!

Located in Olaroz Salt Flat in Jujuy, Argentina, the project addresses a landscape shared by lithium extraction and increasing environmental fragility. Instead of opposing industry, the proposal turns existing infrastructures into regeneration catalysts, transforming the territory into a diversified productive ecosystem.

Architecture: Ezequiel Lopez, María Victoria Echegaray, Agustina Durandez, Universidad Nacional de Córdoba

ECR Health Care Complex

Wave 4 & 5 at the ECR Health Care Complex organize laboratories, clinics, and hospital spaces within a series of volumes shaped by coastal references. The design prioritizes patient comfort, integrating medical functions within environments that reduce the perception of institutional space. Perforated aluminum panels wrap the façades, controlling light and ventilation, while prefabricated balconies and landscaped areas connect the buildings to the surrounding site.

Architecture: FAAB

Rockpool House

Rockpool House is designed as a coastal retreat that steps down a sloping site between bush and beach in Avoca. The layout organizes spaces to frame ocean and landscape views while maintaining a balance between sheltered and open areas. Solid forms, natural materials, and covered terraces define the architecture, supporting a continuous relationship between interior and exterior spaces.

Architecture: Architecture Saville Isaacs
